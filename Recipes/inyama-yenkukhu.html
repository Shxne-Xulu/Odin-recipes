Coq au Vin

This classic French dish of tender, slow-cooked chicken is a timeless comfort food rich in flavour

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 chicken legs
  • 150g smoked lardons
  • 12 round shallots peeled
  • 3 garlic cloves finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato purée
  • a few thyme sprigs
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 175ml red wine
  • 750ml chicken stock
  • 250g button mushrooms halved
  • a handful flat-leaf parsley leaves chopped

Directions

  1. Step 1:Heat the oil in a large pan or casserole, season the chicken legs well and add to the pan. Cook for 4-5 minutes, turning halfway, until browned. Transfer the chicken to a plate.
  2. Step 2:Tip the lardons and shallots into the pan and cook for 5-6 minutes until they brown slightly.
  3. Step 3:Add the chopped garlic and cook for a minute, before adding the tomato purée, thyme and bay leaves, and cooking for a minute more.
  4. Step 4:Add the plain flour and cook for another minute.
  5. Step 5:Pour in the red wine and chicken stock, add the mushrooms, then return the chicken legs to the pan and bring to the boil.
  6. Step 6:Turn down the heat to a simmer and bubble gently for 1 hour until the sauce has thickened slightly and the chicken is meltingly tender. Sprinkle over the chopped parsley and serve.
